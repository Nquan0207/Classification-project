📊 Classification Metrics Formulas

Accuracy
Accuracy = TP + TN TP + TN + FP + FN = Correct Predictions Total Predictions

Overall correctness across all classes.

Precision
Precision = TP TP + FP = True Positives True Positives + False Positives

Proportion of predicted positives that are correct.

Recall
Recall = TP TP + FN = True Positives True Positives + False Negatives

Proportion of actual positives identified correctly.

F1-Score
F1 = 2 x Precision x Recall Precision + Recall = 2TP 2TP + FP + FN

Harmonic mean between precision and recall.

Top-5 Accuracy
Top-5 Accuracy = Correct Top-5 Predictions Total Predictions

A prediction is counted correct if true label appears in top 5 outputs.

📈 Averaging Methods

Macro Average
Macro-Metric = 1 N x Σ(Metric_i)

Treats every class equally, regardless of class size.

Weighted Average
Weighted-Metric = Σ(Metric_i x Support_i) Σ(Support_i)

Weights each class metric by number of true instances.

⚡ Resource Metrics

FLOPs
FLOPs_total = Σ(FLOPs_layer), with FLOPs_layer ≈ 2 x MACs

Approximate compute cost for one forward pass.

🔍 Attention Maps

Wrong-Case Sample

1. Most Attended Sentence (Weight ≈ 0.195):

"[3]: After the Inspector General served the Corporation Counsel with a subpoena for the documents and the Corporation Counsel refused to provide them, the Inspector General retained private counsel and brought suit against the Corporation Counsel to compel production of the unredacted documents pursuant to the subpoena."

Explanation: The model heavily focuses on this sentence because it encapsulates the primary legal conflict and actions.

2. Ignored but Crucial Sentence (Weight ≈ 0.020):

"[41]: On November 4, 2009, through private counsel, the Inspector General in his official capacity filed suit against the Corporation Counsel in her official capacity, seeking a declaratory judgment, a writ of mandamus, and enforcement of the subpoena..."

Explanation: Key civil-litigation terms received low attention, causing the civil case to be misclassified as Other.

Correct-Case Sample

1. The Most Attended Sentence (Weight ≈ 0.780):

"[1]: Clair county allowed certain claims against the estate of Samuel Scott, the plaintiff's intestate, in favor of Crow & Tevis, the defendants in error."

Explanation: The model's attention is overwhelmingly concentrated on this single sentence. It successfully latched onto highly discriminative terminology specific to probate law, namely "estate" and "intestate" (dying without a legal will). Note that the text tokenizer erroneously split the word "St. Clair" across sentences 0 and 1, yet the model perfectly captured the semantic core in sentence 1. This extreme keyword concentration allowed the model to confidently and correctly predict the Probate label.

2. The Ignored but Crucial Sentence (Weight ≈ 0.000):

"[48]: The judgment of the circuit court is reversed, with costs, and the cause remanded for further proceedings."

Explanation: Consistent with the behavior observed in Sample 1, the model completely ignores the final judicial disposition of the case. This suggests that the Bi-LSTM primarily acts as a semantic feature extractor targeting domain-specific vocabularies rather than comprehending the sequential legal outcome. However, in this instance, the strong thematic vocabulary in the early sentences was sufficient for accurate classification.
